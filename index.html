😱 Worst Nightmares — the three tiers
Search a Pokémon to see its live board — the tiers are computed per-mon, per-league.
💀 Tier 1 — Hard Counters. Type advantage plus real shield pressure: a cheap bait and fast energy. They force your shields early, then close. These are the ones that actually beat you.
🪨 Tier 2 — Grinders. They outlast you and convert it. No type advantage needed — a bulky wall that resists you and chips away is still a nightmare. Slow, and inevitable.
🎲 Tier 3 — Coincidental. Super-effective on paper, but no engine behind it. Beatable if you shield correctly.
Reading a card: ⚡ shield pressure (1.00 = average; higher = forces shields faster) · 💥 damage per energy · ⏱ real seconds to charge their cheapest move · and the kit they actually run, with the fast move's turn length (1T–5T).
⏳ Turn parity — the Rouge rule. Two fast moves with identical energy-per-second are not the same weapon. Energy only lands when a fast move finishes, and mid-move you can't act — so a 1-turn move is a scalpel, a 4-turn move is a commitment. Bug Bite and Splash both make 6 energy/sec; Bug Bite reaches a 40-energy move two full turns sooner. The engine now sees the difference. (Named for the one who caught it.)
🗂 Tap any nightmare to open it as a stacked tab over your work — your squad stays exactly where it is. ← minimizes it (it's recoverable), × closes it. Both need a short press-and-hold, so you can't nuke a tab by accident. Esc always backs out.
🧬 IVs & CP — you only need one of them
Search a Pokémon first — this section lives under its analysis.
IVs are optional. It starts you on a realistic 8/11/11 spread, so you can ignore this entirely and still get honest numbers.
🔢 Only know your CP? Tap "I only know my CP" and enter CP + level instead. It reverse-solves your IV spread from the real CP formula. If several spreads hit the same CP (common), it tells you how many and shows the most typical.
It corrects you. Wrong level? It sweeps all 101 half-levels and auto-fixes it. Impossible CP for that species? It says so. Typo? It offers a "did you mean…".
Skip near-perfect IVs in capped leagues. A low Attack IV (0–5) with high Def/HP usually outperforms 15/15/15 — it levels further before the CP cap, meaning more real bulk. Does not apply to Master League, where 15/15/15 is genuinely correct.
🧩 Squad builder & the Command Deck
⠿ Reordering: tap the handle on any squad member — it jumps to the top slot, and whoever was there shifts down one (the bottom wraps to the end). On desktop you can drag it instead.
The Command Deck (the bar under the search box) scrolls sideways — swipe it. Add / ⚡ Analyze / League / Clear squad all live there. A › appears on the right when there's more to reach.
⚡ Analyze unlocks at 2+ Pokémon and reads your squad against this league's power scale — Master League isn't judged by Great League's rules.
Add a mon and the keyboard comes straight back up, cursor blinking, ready for the next one. Dismiss it with the ⌄ in the search box, or just swipe down with your thumb.
📱 Gestures & the app itself
🎣 Pull to cast. Drag down from the top — properly, it's meant to resist — and release to refresh. It's a fishing line, not a button.
Scroll down fast and the search box whips away like a tablecloth; the Command Deck stays put. Scroll up and it snaps back.
